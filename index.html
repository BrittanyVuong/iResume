Brittany Vuong

Biology

I was born and raised in San Gabriel Valley, California. It is a small town that really allowed me to get close to my neighbors. It i also a place full of Asian culture, which I appreciate as a Chinese-Vietnamese American. The amount of food that we can order for pick up that tastes so similar to what my grandma makes is really comforting. Going to another community for college really let me appreciate that even more. Grew up loving animals and taking care of my dog when he was sick. I always wanted to find a career being able to help people understand the animals around them a little better. I find it interesting because we cannot directly communicate with animals, so studying their behavior can help us live in the world together a little better. I will never get over the idea of helping an owner help their family member. I applied to Riverside with a biology major because I was hoping these classes would show much a larger variety of perspectives. The world is made of many different interesting topics and ways to learn.

Picking a carrier this early in life is extremely difficult. I felt like I had commitment issues when choosing a major but luckily i was able to find something that could expand into other topics. The pandemic really made me feel better about my choice because I learn a lot about viruses in class. Although the workload is time consuming, at least it is something I am interested in. I have made a lot of my close friends through being a bio major. We bond over the large workload. All the late nights will be worth it when the degree gets us into great careers.

I think my strengths include being inclusive as well as working well with teammates. My weaknesses include being too indecisive and having too much self doubt to be confindent in some of my decisions.
